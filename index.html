My contribution

The idea
 In the beginning of the semester we were a group of three and the idea was to create a furniture. After one of the team members decided to quit Kristján came up with the idea of making an ukulele.

Planning
 Because of Covid-19 we had to do all the planning online, which we did using Google Hangouts and Facebook Messenger. We decided that Kristján would design the body and I would design the neck using this design for reference. The rest of the project, such as BOM and simulations, will be done by the both of us.

Due to the current crisis we are unable to finish the project and make the ukulele but we are hopeful that we will be able to finish everything once Fab Lab opens again.

Design - Ukulele Neck
 The design was made in Fusion 360. I started by watching few Youtube videos to get ideas on how to make the neck and google ukulele images to get an idea for the design. I made sure that the dimension that I used for the bottom of the neck were the same as on the design that we had decided to use for reference. This was crucial so that the neck and body would fit together.

I started by drawing a simple design in Fusion 360:
 
When the outlines were ready I used the extrude function to create the neck.

The dimensions on the bottom part of the neck fit the body that Kristján designed so we are able to connect the two designs.


I added holes on the design for the tuners and string trees using the hole function.

To be able to attach the string trees I created two holes with 14 mm diameter that had 6.5 mm depth. Inside of them I created two other holes that had 6.35 mm diameter.


When the holes had been added the neck design was ready.
